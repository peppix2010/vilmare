A charity case study for a course project

A redesign of Farm School International's existing website.
Farm School International is a Social Enterprise founded at the beginning of 2018 in Birmingham. Their website is a little bit confusing, the homepage is structured with lots of information, not giving the best user experience to neither of the 3 user target groups: Unemployed Adults, Clients and Partners.

Tools | XD, Illustrator, Photoshop

More info about the case

Who the case is about
Farm School International is a Social Enterprise founded at the beginning of 2018 in Birmingham.
What they do
They provide vocational education, training and work experience through a hands-on programme in horticulture project management to unemployed adults (18+). They encourage and support community initiatives in food security, conservation and habitat beautification.
How they do it
They offer a full time training program over a 12-weeks period which coincides with the school term. It covers all the skills required to profit from a business in horticulture, the theory, the practice and how to manage business risks.
Where they do it
-The Hub: classroom and laboratory.
-Community Extension Units: a platform on which learners and customers from the community are brought together to work on a project. All CEU activities contribute to the learner's work experience and are managed by learner teams supervised by the CEU senior technician.
Audience
1. Unemployed Adults (18+). Average age is 18-25.
2. Clients – Individuals that need their gardens maintained.
3. Partners – Organisations that need garden/grounds maintenance.
What is needed
A simplification of the current website http://www.farmschool-international.co.uk/ with a focus on inspiring unemployed adults to join.
PROCESS

Persona development

We have considered 4 aspects to define a persona for each of the 3 target groups:
- Knowledge (how they get to know about the website)
- Context (where they use it)
- Problems (which problems they have, what they need)
- Motivation (inner motivations that drive them to use The Farm School website)
Activity Group to define a persona and a customer journey for the 1° Target Group: Young Adult Unemployed.
Details...
After the Activity I have developed a Persona profile for the user Unemployed Adult.

The 4 aspects we considered to define a persona

Knowledge: we have supposed this user gets to know about the Farm School through word of mouth (friends, parents, previous students) or referred by college/school or referred by job/career advice centres or wall advertisements(garden stores, library) or social media or meet-ups events.
Context: we have supposed the user discovers about the Farm school while he's on the web on his mobile phone at home, or he's in a cafe', or on the bus; at home while he's researching opportunities on the computer; after school programs and activities; at the library or job centre; while on holiday with friends.
Problems:the user could be facing the difficulty to find a job, he could feel demotivated, have negative thoughts, have no work experience, he could be confused about his career, about what to do in the future, he could be psychological stuck due to depression or mental illness, there could be a lack of advice/guidance, a lack of funds.
Motivation: while there could be several motivations we need to focus only to the one we think is the most important and keep this in mind while we think to the customer journey and design the website – we choose the need to improve on something.

Customer Journey

Goal: Successful application

Same process has been followed to define the other two users groups: clients and partners.
We have decided to focus on the user Unemployed Adult because he is the main target of the design of Farm School International website.

User Flow

In a Task activity group to better define a User Flow, we have considered all the possible tasks the student wants to accomplish when he interacts with the websites.
First of all we have divided the Tasks in different time phases: Find out - Apply - Study - Work
I have focused on the Student enrolled to the course, in the time phase Study. This is the User Flow.
